KindBridge369 · Privacy and Boundaries

Help without taking over.

KindBridge is built for plain-language support, printable worksheets, and verified local discovery. It should protect dignity, consent, and privacy at every step.

What KindBridge does

  • Helps name a need in simple words.
  • Suggests next steps and things to gather.
  • Encourages verified local details.
  • Creates printable notes people can keep.

What KindBridge does not do

  • It does not replace trained professional help.
  • It does not promise eligibility or outcomes.
  • It does not require accounts, payments, or tracking.
  • It does not need private details to be useful.

Consent rule

Ask before writing, calling, sharing, or storing someone else’s details. A helper can offer support without owning the person’s situation.

Minimum notes rule

Write only what helps the next step. Avoid extra private details. Use initials when a full name is not needed.

Verification rule

Local resources change. Mark the date checked, the contact path, and any unknowns. Do not fill gaps by guessing.

Helper humility

The best helper slows the room down, keeps choices with the person, and leaves a clear next step instead of pressure.
